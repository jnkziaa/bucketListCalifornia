Lake Tahoe is the third deepest lake in North America and the tenth deepest in the world.
The lake is 22 miles in length and 12 miles wide with approximately 72 miles of shoreline.
Its greatest measured depth is 1,645 feet. The average depth of the lake is 1,000 feet.
Another amazing fact about Lake Tahoe is that the bottom of the lake, 4,580 feet in elevation,
is actually lower than the Carson Valley floor in Nevada.
